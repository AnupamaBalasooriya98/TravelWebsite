Dancing Tradition




There are three dance traditions remain in Sri Lanka. They are,

  • Kandyan dance tradition (Ves dance)
  • Sabaragamuwa dance tradition
  • Low country dance tradition

Among these three, Kandyan dance is very very important, because it is considered sacred.




According to the legend, it was introduced to Sri Lanka from the region called Malayarata in India, origins of the dance lies in dance ritual is known as KOHOMBA KANKARIYA. It was performed first, by the two brothers of king of Malayarata on that era. There are three shamans came to Sri Lanka to cure the who were suffering with the mysterious illness of the king Panduwasu Dewa who ruled the country at that time. After the performance of dance illness vanished.





The costume is dressed by the dancer is called Ves costume. That is the reason to nominate this dance ritual as Ves dance. This outfit is very important and considered sacred. It consist lot of arrays. Particularly head gear is considered sacred and believed it is belonging to the deity kohomba.



The originally it was encompassed to vicinity of Kandy. Otherwise this dance ritual was originally performed by the identical dancers who were separated caste under the Kandyan feudal system and who were dedicated to temple of tooth in Kandy. They had a significant role to play in Kandy perahera (pageant)that is held by the temple of tooth once a year. This is the reason nominates Kandyan dance.
